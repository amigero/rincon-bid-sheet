How the ceiling is built: the max bid is the highest hammer price where bid + IAA buyer fee + internet fee + $140 fixed + recon + pack + transport + holding + margin ≤ retail. IAA fees come straight from the Nov-2024 Standard-Volume schedule — the buyer fee is bracketed by hammer price (e.g. a $3,450 hammer carries $655 buyer + $110 internet + $140 fixed = $905 all-in), so the ceiling already absorbs them. The +fees line under each max bid is what you'll actually owe IAA on top of the hammer. Retail = rebuilt/salvage-title AZ resale, not clean value. Sales tax is excluded — resale inventory is TPT-exempt once you give IAA your AZ resale certificate, so don't bake it into the bid.

The pack toggle only appears for NiMH hybrids (Prius / RAV4H / Highlander / Crosstrek) you can rebuild from sourced modules — check it to bake in the worst case. Teslas, the Leaf, the Kia Niro and the 2025 Civic Hybrid run Li-ion packs with no cheap rebuild; their notes say so and you bid to worst case regardless.

Retail and recon figures are my starting estimates — overwrite them with your own comps and shop numbers. Current bids are as of the sale list export and will climb live Thursday.
